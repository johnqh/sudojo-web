Naked Single

When there is only one candidate for a particular cell, it is called naked single.

Let's look at the example:

 

R3C9 can only be 9, since 1, 2, 3 and 5 exists in Column 9, 5 and 8 exist in Row 3, and 3, 4, 6 and 7 exist in Block 3.

To spot Naked Single, often you have to look at all three Houses (Row, Column, and Block).
