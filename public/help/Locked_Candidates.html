Locked Candidates

Locked Candidates is an advanced single-digit technique to remove candidates from cells. Pencilmark is required.

If a candidate in House (Row, Column or Block) 1 can only exist in the interception with House 2, you can remove the candidate from the other cells in House 2.

Locked Candidates only happens with a Block and either a Row or a Column.

Let's look at the example:

In Block 6, candidate 2 can only exist in the interception between Block 6 and Column 9.

Either R4C9 is 2, or R5C9 is 2, or R6C9 is 2. 

In any case, R7C9 cannot be 2.
